  SMART KEYBOARD  v1.0.0  —  User Manual

WHAT IS SMART KEYBOARD?
------------------------
Smart Keyboard is a desktop tool that sits quietly in your
system tray and helps you fix grammar or translate text into
Indian languages — without leaving whatever app you are using.

Select any text, press a hotkey, and Smart Keyboard instantly
shows a popup with the corrected or translated result. One more
click pastes it back.


GETTING STARTED
---------------
1. Double-click SmartKeyboard.exe to launch the app.

2. You will see a keyboard icon appear in the system tray
   (bottom-right corner of your screen, near the clock).

3. That's it — the app is running in the background.


HOW TO USE IT
-------------
1. Open any app (Word, browser, WhatsApp Web, Notepad, etc.)

2. Select the text you want to fix or translate.

3. Press  Ctrl + Alt + K  (the default hotkey).

4. A popup appears with your selected text already loaded.

5. Click  "Fix Grammar"  or  "Translate"  to process it.

6. Review the result, then click  "Paste"  to replace your
   original text automatically.


GRAMMAR vs TRANSLATE MODE
--------------------------
Inside the popup, you can switch between two modes:

  Grammar   — Fixes spelling, grammar, and sentence structure.
              Works best with English text.

  Translate — Translates text into your chosen Indian language
              (Hindi, Telugu, Tamil, Bengali, and more).

The mode you last used is remembered the next time you open
the popup.


CHANGING SETTINGS
-----------------
Right-click the tray icon  →  select "Settings"

From Settings you can change:

  Default Language  — The language Translate mode uses by default.
                      You can still change it per-session inside
                      the popup as well.

  Hotkey            — Click inside the capture box and press your
                      preferred key combination. Must include at
                      least one of  Ctrl / Shift / Alt  plus a
                      letter or number.
                      Example: Ctrl+Shift+H

  Automate Mode     — When ON, the app processes your selected text
                      immediately on hotkey press and pastes the
                      result without showing the full popup.
                      A small circle appears — click it to review
                      or undo the change.
                      When OFF (default), the popup always opens
                      so you can review before pasting.

Click  Save  to apply.


SUPPORTED LANGUAGES
-------------------
  Hindi       Bengali     Marathi     Telugu
  Tamil       Kannada     Punjabi     Malayalam


AUTOMATE MODE — QUICK GUIDE
----------------------------
1. Enable Automate Mode in Settings.
2. Select text in any app and press the hotkey.
3. The text is fixed/translated and pasted back instantly.
4. A small circle appears on screen — click it to see what
   changed or to undo.


QUITTING THE APP
----------------
Right-click the tray icon  →  select "Quit"

The app does not appear in the taskbar — it only lives in
the system tray. If you cannot find the tray icon, click the
small arrow ( ^ ) near the clock to show hidden icons.


TIPS
----
- The popup closes automatically when you click away.
  Press the hotkey again to reopen it.

- You can change the language inside the popup for a single
  session without touching Settings.

- If the popup does not appear, make sure the app is still
  running (check the system tray).

- The app starts only when you launch SmartKeyboard.exe.
  If you want it to start with Windows, enable that option
  in Settings.


TROUBLESHOOTING
---------------
Hotkey not working?
  → Another app may be using the same hotkey.
    Change it in Settings (right-click tray icon).

Popup appears but processing fails?
  → The AI models may not have loaded yet.
    Wait a few seconds after launch and try again.

Text not pasting automatically?
  → Click inside the target app first to give it focus,
    then press the hotkey.

App not visible in tray?
  → Click the  ^  arrow near the clock to show hidden icons.
    You may need to drag the Smart Keyboard icon out to keep
    it always visible.
